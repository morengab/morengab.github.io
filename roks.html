Our Kubernetes design team returned from KubeCon Seattle in the wake of IBM’s $34 billion acquisition of Red Hat. We soon found out we’d have the chance to design the first integration of these two brands as our product team began work on a managed version of Red Hat’s flagship product, the OpenShift Container Platform, to be offered on IBM Cloud. As part of the design team, I helped with everything from user research, to concepting, crits, and data analysis using AI.

Designing ROKS, was a fascinating experience for me, it let me see first hand the huge benefit that a managed cloud service can bring to IT infrastructure teams. We learned it was not uncommon for full teams to be staffed in order to install, run and maintain OpenShift environments. Through offering easy install, IBM-management, and one-click updates, we were essentially letting clients focus their time and money on their core business.

As our team worked through the Closed Beta, I was able to use Watson NLP to analyze our Slack channels, surface and address trends as part of our continuous improvement.

Red Hat OpenShift on IBM Cloud launched right as IBM closed it’s landmark acquisition of Red Hat. It powered the Masters in its first year.
